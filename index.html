The truth is

The age of the personal brand is here.

It's always been true that people buy from people. But now? The brave new world of personal branding is raw, real, and oh-so-satisfying.

You know your work changes people. You know your impact is mighty. But your brand doesn't show that right now.

What branding experts don't commonly share is that the work of personal branding is deeply, well, personal. You can upgrade your logo, yes, you can get new brand photos, sure. But if you want your brand to accurately reflect your greatness? To point the way forward, for many years to come?

It's going to require you to dig deep into what's true, real, and powerfully relevant.

You can't outsource your creative thinking on your personal brand to AI, your VA, or even a marketing agency. People aren't mind readers. You are the expert in yourself.

We need real leaders now more than ever.

In the age of 'alternative facts', we need to see the person behind the persona. We don't hire someone because they look polished. We hire people who share the stories that inform their thinking, who walk their talk, who create out loud.

Powerful brands convey your beliefs, opinions, attitude instantly, in one image, one sentence, one glance.

Far too many female founders are victims of their socialisation. Especially in Australia, we're conditioned to be modest, relatable, and easygoing. We don't want to brag, nor big-note ourselves. We've seen other women online get torn down for far less.

You know that trying to be 'professional' is self-censorship, right? And that placating LinkedIn in pantyhose is a recipe for fakery and self-loathing. Playing it safe is the riskiest thing you could do right now.

Perhaps you feel like you're too old, or it's too late for you. You see young, conventionally attractive, put together women online, and relegate yourself to "has been".

Maybe you've outgrown your image. Your profile photos are (ahem!) several years out-of-date, and your brand feels tired, irrelevant, or misaligned with who you are now.

Perhaps you're playing it safe with branding and marketing that blends in. You know you could be bolder, but you're hesitant to attract the judgement of clients, peers, family and friends.

Maybe you're a deep thinker who's repelled by the performative vulnerability you see other women in business doing. You're a little obsessed with accurately representing yourself. You're prone to overthinking, and your new brand? Well, let's just say, it's an ongoing work in progress — and has been for longer than you care to admit.

Perhaps you're in the thick of change, in that liminal space between 'not this' and 'not that'. You're unclear about who you are in this state of flux, and your marketing has become almost mute.

It's not your audience

A Powerful Brand
Pulls Your Audience In

Image

Far too many business owners blame their audience for low visibility or engagement. They believe they have a lead generation problem, rather than a branding problem.

But it's not your audience that's the problem — it's your brand. A powerful brand acts like a magnet to pull people towards you. Your best, most qualified leads are likely right in front of you. But they can't see you when your brand isn't working.

The more your brand represents and reflects you, the better it'll work to magnetise the right people to you.

We're at a most interesting time in business right now, where character, integrity, credibility matter more than ever. Too many scammers and grifters online have dented people's trust, and an avalanche of AI-generated slop is undermining our perception of what's real, and what's fantasy.

Owners, leaders and legends with high integrity get caught up in overthinking and imposter syndrome and cede market share to others who are bolder, louder, and clearer with their brand.

You don't need a personality change, or to dye your hair pink, or have a frontal lobotomy to become a Category of One brand. You just need to turn up the volume.

We're here to shake things up, make meaning for others, demonstrate leadership, and change the trajectory of the future. We don't need more women with beige brands. We don't need to seek permission, or solicit advice from those unqualified to give it.

What would change in your life, if you finally felt like your brand accurately represented you — not you of ten years ago, or even five years — but the bleeding edge of what you're growing into?

This we know for sure: far too many highly experienced, (over) qualified women-in-business are routinely ignored. Way too many legitimate experts are overlooked because their brand is misaligned, outdated, or beige. Countless female founders are hiding in plain sight because their marketing is boring, ad hoc, or only done when desperate.

How do we know? Because we've seen this over and over again. And, we've lived it too.

Meet your hosts

Who Are We?

We're Brook McCarthy and Ellissa Nagle, two branding experts who've worked with innumerable women in our own businesses, over the last 35+ years of combined experience.

We met on the internet, both part of a Facebook group filled with legendary women-in-business. Lis' dry humour caught Brook's eye, and she filled out a form on Lis's website, requesting to meet for dinner in Brisbane, on her next trip. Much to the surprise of Lis's introverted heart, she said yes. We bonded over good food and fonts at Gerard's in the Valley, and have been friends ever since.

Over many years, between many cocktails in Brisbane, Sydney and on Zoom, we've gone deep into what it means to build a strong, authentic personal brand. A brand that walks its talk, in alignment with the whole person. A brand which is shareable. A brand which evolves with the business and owner. A brand which endures as changes accelerate.

We want to see women in business become outrageously successful through taking their reputation building seriously. We're done seeing female founders who are hugely experienced, intelligent, and funny misrepresent themselves with brands that are beige, out-of-date, or same-same-not-different.
